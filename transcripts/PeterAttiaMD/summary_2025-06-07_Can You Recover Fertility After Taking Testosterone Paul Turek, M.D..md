This clip is from episode 351 ‒ Male fertility: optimizing reproductive health, diagnosing and treating infertility, and navigating testosterone replacement therapy | Paul Turek, M.D.

In this clip, they discuss:

- Exogenous Testosterone Suppresses Sperm Production – Even after a few months, testosterone therapy (injection, gel, oral, or nasal) typically shuts down sperm production
- Some Testosterone Forms May Be Less Inhibitory – Intranasal or oral testosterone taken in multiple daily doses may allow some sperm production
- Long-Term Use Can Lead to Complete Suppression – Men taking testosterone for years, especially via weekly injections, often present with zero sperm
- Recovery Is Possible but Depends on Duration and Dosage – Shorter-term, cyclical use can often be reversed; long-term, constant use increases the risk of permanent shutdown
