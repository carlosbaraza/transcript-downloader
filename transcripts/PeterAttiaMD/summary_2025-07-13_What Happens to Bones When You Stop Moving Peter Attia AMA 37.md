In this clip, they discuss:

- Disuse Osteopenia Explained – Bone loss occurs when bones are unloaded, as seen in spaceflight and long-term immobilization
- The Risk of Bed Rest – Prolonged bed rest (e.g., in pregnancy or injury recovery) can contribute to rapid bone density decline
- Rates of Bone Decline – Bone loss can occur at 2% per month in partial paralysis and up to 7% in complete paralysis
- Load What You Can – Even in bed, loading muscles through physical therapy or resistance can help prevent deterioration
- Mouse Study on Bone Loss – Research on mice shows bisphosphonates can counteract bone loss caused by immobility
