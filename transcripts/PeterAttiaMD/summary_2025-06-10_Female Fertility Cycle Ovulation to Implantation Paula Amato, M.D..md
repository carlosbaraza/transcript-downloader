In this clip, they discuss:

- Finite Eggs from Birth – Women are born with all the eggs they'll ever have, peaking at 2 million in the womb and dropping to 400,000 by puberty
- Hormones Drive Egg Selection – FSH and LH stimulate growth of follicles; one dominant follicle is selected (somewhat randomly) for ovulation
- The chosen egg is released, travels into the fallopian tube, and may meet sperm for fertilization, typically on day 14 of the cycle
- Barriers to Conception – Sperm must reach the egg, but fertilization only allows one to penetrate before a “force field” blocks others
- Implantation Timeline – After fertilization, the embryo divides over 5–6 days before implanting in the uterus—if the egg is chromosomally normal
