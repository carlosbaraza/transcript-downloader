## Description

Tanuj Nakra & Suzan Obagi, two leading experts in aesthetic medicine, explore the science of facial aging & skin health as well as the responsibilities physicians face in the world of cosmetic procedures. They examine the biological & hormonal drivers of facial aging, the evolutionary & psychological foundations of attractiveness, & the impact of modern media on beauty standards. They discuss the rise in cosmetic procedures among adolescents & the ethical responsibilities of physicians in these cases. The conversation then shifts to a practical, evidence-based guide to skin health—covering the use of mineral sunscreens, retinoids, vitamin C, & tailored skincare routines, as well as the management of acne & the psychology behind aesthetic consultations. Additionally, they assess of Peter’s own face, offering a revealing look at what aesthetic medicine can achieve & how to think critically about pursuing it.
