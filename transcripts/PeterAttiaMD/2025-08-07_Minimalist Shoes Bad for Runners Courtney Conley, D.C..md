In this clip, they discuss:

- Foot Strength Varies by Individual – Transitioning to minimalist shoes depends on a person’s injury and trauma history
- Start Slow with Minimalist Transitions – Patients begin with 5–10 minutes of wear to assess comfort before reducing stack height
- Avoid Rushing the Transition – Moving too quickly from cushioned to minimalist shoes often results in discomfort and abandonment
-  Foot Strike Type Isn’t What Matters Most – The key isn't how the foot strikes, but where it strikes relative to the body
- Overstriding Is the Real Issue – A foot that lands too far ahead of the body increases ground reaction force and injury risk
