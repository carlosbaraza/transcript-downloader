This clip is from episode Navigating bone health: early life influences and advanced strategies for improvement and injury prevention (#214 rebroadcast)

In this clip, they discuss:

- Osteoporosis Can Be Silent – Many individuals don’t realize they have low bone density until they experience a fracture
- Genetic & Lifestyle Influences – Family history, poor nutrition, smoking, and sedentary behavior are significant risk factors
- Corticosteroids Weaken Bones – Chronic use of steroids like prednisone can impair bone formation and increase fracture risk
-  Hormonal Changes Matter – In women, estrogen loss post-menopause rapidly accelerates bone loss; testosterone also plays a role for men
- Low Body Weight Increases Risk – Individuals with low BMI or low body fat often have less bone mass to begin with
